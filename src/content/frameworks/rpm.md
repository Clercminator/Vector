# Rapid Planning Method (RPM)

The Rapid Planning Method (RPM) is a system designed to help you focus on the outcome you want, rather than just the to-do list. It turns your focus from "what do I have to do" to "what do I want to achieve".

## History

Developed by Tony Robbins to manage his massive schedule and business empire. He realized traditional to-do lists just led to more stress, not fulfillment.

## Who is this for?

High achievers who want to ensure their daily actions align with their life's purpose and want to feel fulfilled, not just busy.

## How to Apply It

1. **capture.** Write down everything you need to do (brain dump).
2. **chunk.** Group similar items together.
3. **create your RPM block.** For each chunk, ask:
    - **Result (R):** What do I really want? (The Output)
    - **Purpose (P):** Why do I want it? (The Driver/Leverage)
    - **Massive Action Plan (M):** What specific actions will I take?

## Pros & Cons

### Pros
- Connects tasks to purpose
- high motivation
- prevents "empty" busyness

### Cons
- Learning curve
- Can be overkill for simple tasks
- Requires emotional engagement
