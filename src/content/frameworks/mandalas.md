# Mandala Chart

## What is it?

The Mandala Chart is a planning framework that places one central goal in the middle of a 3x3 grid, then expands that goal into eight surrounding themes. Each of those themes becomes the center of its own 3x3 grid, producing a total of **64 supporting actions or subgoals**.

## The core structure

1. **Center goal:** The main outcome you want to reach.
2. **Eight focus areas:** The domains that most influence the goal.
3. **Eight actions per focus area:** Specific behaviors, skills, or milestones that make each domain tangible.

## Why it matters

Many ambitious goals stay abstract because they are too large to hold in working memory. The Mandala Chart makes a complex ambition visible all at once. It is useful for personal reinvention, career planning, and multidimensional projects where one linear checklist is not enough.

## How to use it

1. Write one precise central goal in the middle.
2. Choose eight categories that meaningfully support that goal.
3. Expand each category into eight concrete actions, habits, or milestones.
4. Review the chart to spot gaps, dependencies, and imbalance.
5. Convert the chart into shorter execution cycles so it stays usable.

## Best use cases

- Long-range personal growth plans
- Career reinvention with multiple moving parts
- Startup or creative projects with cross-functional needs
- Any goal that requires balance across skills, habits, and relationships
