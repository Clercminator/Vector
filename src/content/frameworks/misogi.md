# The Misogi Challenge

## What is it?

The Misogi Challenge is a modern adaptation of an ancient Japanese Shinto purification ritual. In its modern form, popularized by Dr. Marcus Elliott, it involves taking on one defining challenge each year.

## The Two Rules

1. **50% Chance of Success:** The challenge must be so difficult that you truly believe you might fail. If you are sure you can do it, it's not a Misogi.
2. **Don't Die:** Safety is the only boundary. The goal is to push your limits, not to be reckless.

## Why do it?

We live in a "comfort crisis". We rarely test our true limits. A Misogi "resets" your baseline for what is difficult. After you've spent 12 hours paddling across an ocean or climbing a mountain, a "hard day at the office" feels trivial.

## How to execute

1. **Define the Challenge:** Pick something unique to you.
2. **Plan the Prep:** You can train, but you can't guarantee success.
3. **Go:** Do it.
4. **Reflect:** Whether you succeed or fail, the value is in the attempt.
