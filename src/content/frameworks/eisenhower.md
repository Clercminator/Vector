# Eisenhower Matrix

The Eisenhower Matrix helps you decide on and prioritize tasks by urgency and importance, sorting out less urgent and important tasks which you should either delegate or not do at all.

## History

Attributed to Dwight D. Eisenhower, the 34th President of the United States, known for his incredible productivity. He famously said, "What is important is seldom urgent, and what is urgent is seldom important."

## Who is this for?

Great for people constantly fighting fires, dealing with urgent/client demands, or struggling to find time for long-term goals.

## How to Apply It

Categorize tasks into 4 quadrants:

1. **Do (Urgent & Important):** Crises, deadlines. Do these now.
2. **Schedule (Important, Not Urgent):** Strategic planning, exercise, relationship building. Schedule these.
3. **Delegate (Urgent, Not Important):** Mails, calls, interruptions. Delegate these if possible.
4. **Delete (Neither):** Time wasters, social media scrolling. Eliminate these.

## Pros & Cons

### Pros
- Clear validation of priorities
- Helps reduce stress
- Encourages delegation

### Cons
- hard to determine "importance" sometimes
- urgency is often subjective
- doesn't account for energy levels
