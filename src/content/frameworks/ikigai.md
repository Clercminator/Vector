# Ikigai

## What is it?

Ikigai (生き甲斐) is a Japanese concept meaning "reason for being" or "that which makes life worth living." Your ikigai lies at the intersection of four circles: **what you love** (passion), **what you're good at** (profession), **what the world needs** (mission), and **what you can be paid for** (vocation). The center—where all four overlap—is your purpose.

## The four circles

1. **What you love:** Activities or topics that make you lose track of time. Passion without obligation.
2. **What you're good at:** Your skills, strengths, and what people ask you for.
3. **What the world needs:** Problems you care about, causes that matter, gaps you see.
4. **What you can be paid for:** Where your skills meet demand—viable work or value exchange.

## Why it matters

When all four overlap, you have a direction that is meaningful, sustainable, and aligned. Ikigai reduces existential drift and helps bridge passion and livelihood. It is not a single job title but a direction you can refine over time.

## How to use it

1. **Reflect on each circle** honestly; avoid mistaking "what you're good at" for "what you love."
2. **Find the intersection** and phrase it as one clear purpose statement.
3. **Revisit as life changes**—ikigai can evolve with you.
