# OKR (Objectives and Key Results)

OKR is a goal-setting framework for defining and tracking objectives and their outcomes. It's about setting ambitious goals and tracking progress with hard data.

## History

Intel's Andy Grove developed it ("iMBOs"), but it was popularized by John Doerr at Google, who used it to scale the company from 40 employees to thousands.

## Who is this for?

Teams and organizations that need alignment and measurable targets. Can also be used for personal yearly goals.

## How to Apply It

1. **Set an Objective (O):** Significant, concrete, action-oriented, and inspirational. (e.g., "Launch an MVP")
2. **Define Key Results (KRs):** 3-5 measurable milestones that tell you if you hit the objective. (e.g., "Get 1000 signups", "Achieve 5% conversion")
3. **Track & Grade:** regularly check-in (weekly/monthly) and grade your progress (0.0 to 1.0).

## Pros & Cons

### Pros
- Creates alignment
- Encourages ambitious goals ("moonshots")
- measurable accountability

### Cons
- Can reduce morale if goals are too hard
- Setting good KRs is difficult
- Can become bureaucratic
